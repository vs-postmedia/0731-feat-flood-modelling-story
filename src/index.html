Many Fraser Valley dikes at risk

NOTE: Each block represents a 100 metre section of dike. Blocks are coloured based on the lowest height measurement within each 100 metre section. The analysis is limited to Fraser River freshet flood scenarios, and does not include potential flooding from other rivers or from coastal storm surge. It does not include other potential causes for dike failure, such as erosion or seepage. The map above shows only a subset of dikes in the Fraser Valley.

  • More than half the dikes in the Fraser Valley would overtop in a repeat of the 1894 spring flood – considered a one-in-500 year event – according to modelling by the Fraser Basin Council.Scroll for more...

  • Floodwaters would overtop red sections of dikes in a flood similar to 1894.

  • Yellow sections would see floodwaters come within 60cm or less of overtopping, placing them at-risk for failure.

  • Sections in green are considered safe from overtopping.
